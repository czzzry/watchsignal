# Locked Visual Direction Summary

## Purpose

This summary captures the visual and product-direction conclusions locked from the high-fidelity concept review on June 28, 2026.
It is the bridge between concept inspiration and real product redesign work.

## Core conclusion

The project should stop aiming for “cleaned-up prototype” quality.
The correct target is a flagship consumer app feel.

That means the redesign should preserve:
- a coherent nighttime cinematic world,
- premium product polish,
- one or two ownable signature moments,
- poster-led drama where it helps decisions,
- and strict readability and usability underneath the visual ambition.

## Locked north-star direction

The approved direction is:

`Premium cinematic mobile product with restrained cyberpunk nightlife soul`

This means:
- not a generic streaming clone,
- not a gamer skin,
- not a neon theme pasted on top of plain app structure,
- not a flat, conservative utility app.

## What must survive from the concept

### 1. A true signature launch moment

The loading sting is the first thing that felt truly special and ownable.
The product should keep a short branded intro/loading moment.

Locked direction.
- Use the TV-static launch sting or a close descendant of it.
- Keep it short and premium.
- Let it carry more personality than the main app screens.
- The current revised static direction is much closer to target, but the exact TV asset and CRT/static treatment should be revisited later for a more specific final finish.

### 2. A flagship-feeling startup screen

The startup screen finally felt like a modern flagship app home screen.

Locked direction.
- Keep the calmer premium shell after the launch sting.
- Keep the date-led confidence and strong next-step clarity.
- Let the main action remain extremely obvious.
- Keep history and diagnostic concerns out of the main path.

### 3. A poster-led reaction card

The reaction card concept finally felt like the right level.

Locked direction.
- Poster art should be a primary cinematic surface.
- The card should feel premium and exciting, not just functional.
- The explanation area should become genuinely helpful, not generic filler.
- Include a fast confidence cue like a Rotten Tomatoes-style score or equivalent.

### 4. A satisfying premium payoff screen

The results reveal felt like the right payoff direction.

Locked direction.
- The best pick must remain the hero.
- The payoff should feel emotionally satisfying and premium.
- The result card should include a plain-language shared-why explanation once LLM support is added.
- The match indicator should visibly respond to recommendation strength.

## Locked interaction and content notes

### Startup controls

These are product-surface notes, not just visual notes.
They should be preserved in later redesign planning.

Locked direction.
- `People` must be adjustable.
- Support at least:
  - `Husband`
  - `Wife`
  - `Husband + Wife`
- `Language` must be adjustable.
- Support at least:
  - `English`
  - `Foreign audio + English subtitles`
  - `No rules`

### Reaction controls

The reaction area needs clearer semantic separation.

Locked direction.
- `Interested / Maybe / No` are tonight-fit actions.
- `Also seen this` is a separate memory action.
- The memory action must not look mutually exclusive with the main reactions.
- It should live in a separate row or clearly separate utility area.

### Result explanation

The result should eventually explain itself in couple-language, not just in score-language.

Locked direction.
- Reserve space for one short shared-why line.
- The line should be plain, human, and specific.
- It should describe the overlap between both people’s taste and the movie’s attributes.

## Visual system rules now locked

### World

- Nighttime cinematic atmosphere.
- Premium first, neon second.
- Distinctive but not costume-like.

### Palette

- Near-black and deep navy base.
- Cyan and violet as main accents.
- Use hotter pink or red sparingly for special emphasis only.

### Typography

- Premium modern sans as the main UI voice.
- Optional stylized accent type only for launch or reveal moments, not normal product reading.

### Materials

- Poster-led layered depth.
- Soft glass used sparingly.
- Controlled glow on special states, not everywhere.

### Controls

- Premium rounded-rectangle controls.
- Strong primary action emphasis.
- No bubbly or toy-like button language.

### Progress

- Move away from chunky multi-pill progress.
- Favor a cleaner progress bar with current-step label.

### Motion

- Cinematic transitions only on key moments.
- Keep dense reading and decision states stable.

## What is still open

These are the most important open visual decisions after the first concept pass.

1. Exactly how cheeky the launch copy should be.
2. How intense the glow and accent lighting should be in real UI.
3. How dramatic the results reveal should feel.
4. How much information the reaction card shows by default versus behind expansion.
5. Whether the match-strength visual should be a heart or another responsive shape.

## What the first redesign implementation slice should now be

The first redesign slice should not be “the whole app.”
It should be the minimum surface that proves the new direction in the real product.

Recommended first redesign slice:
- launch/loading sting,
- startup screen,
- reaction card,
- and the updated reaction control separation for `Also seen this`.

Why this slice first.
- It contains the first emotional impression.
- It contains the most repeated and product-defining interaction.
- It proves whether the concept can survive translation into real UI.

The results screen should follow immediately after as the second redesign slice.

## Plain-English summary

The concept review gave us a real north star.
The product should feel like a top-tier cinematic mobile app, not just a polished prototype.
The most important things to preserve are the ownable launch moment, the flagship-feeling startup, the poster-led reaction card, and the satisfying premium result reveal.
The first real implementation slice should prove those ideas in the startup and reaction flow before we redesign everything else.
